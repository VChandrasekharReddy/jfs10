Scarlett Johansson

Scarlett Johansson is an American actress known for her versatile roles and critically acclaimed performances.

Early Life and Career Beginnings

Johansson first appeared on stage in an off-Broadway play as a child actress. She made her film debut in the fantasy comedy North (1994) and gained early recognition for her roles in Manny & Lo (1996), The Horse Whisperer (1998), and Ghost World (2001).

Transition to Adult Roles

Her shift to adult roles came in 2003 with Lost in Translation, for which she won the BAFTA Award for Best Actress. She continued to gain praise for playing a 17th-century servant in Girl with a Pearl Earring (2003), a troubled teenager in A Love Song for Bobby Long (2004), and a seductress in Match Point (2005).

Collaboration with Woody Allen

In Match Point (2005), Johansson marked her first collaboration with Woody Allen, who later directed her in Scoop (2006) and Vicky Cristina Barcelona (2008). Other notable works include The Prestige (2006) and her music albums Anywhere I Lay My Head (2008) and Break Up (2009), which both charted on the Billboard 200.

Broadway Debut and the Marvel Cinematic Universe

In 2010, Johansson debuted on Broadway in a revival of A View from the Bridge, winning the Tony Award for Best Performance by a Featured Actress in a Play. She began portraying Black Widow in the Marvel Cinematic Universe starting with Iron Man 2, ultimately reprising the role in multiple films, leading up to her solo feature Black Widow (2021), gaining global stardom.

Critically Acclaimed Roles

During this period, Johansson starred in the science fiction films Her (2013), Under the Skin (2013), and Lucy (2014). She received two simultaneous Academy Award nominations—Best Actress and Best Supporting Actress—for her roles in Marriage Story (2019) and Jojo Rabbit (2019), becoming one of the few actors to achieve this feat.
